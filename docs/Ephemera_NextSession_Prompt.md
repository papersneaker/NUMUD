### Session Goals — In Order

**1. AI Storyteller Design**

The GDD has a placeholder in Section 16 noting that the AI Storyteller scope needs a dedicated design session. That is what this session is for.

The AI Storyteller is one of the most critical systems in Ephemera. It needs to handle:

- All seven patron contacts (one per clan) — reactive, persistent, with hidden agendas
- Methuselah stirring effects — escalating environmental weirdness per clan and district
- The Correspondent — the Malkavian patron, who may not be one entity
- Wight management — retired player characters redeployed as hostile NPCs
- Vitae Sanctus / Vitae Corrupta tracking — triggering stirring escalation at thresholds
- Grand Conclave recognition — treating player-called events as Chronicle moments
- Chronicle cycle transitions — archiving ended cycles, converting Elder Fades to new contacts
- Sabbat staged emergence — managing the three-phase Sabbat arc
- Gap-filling between human Storyteller sessions — ambient events, NPC behavior, world flavor
- Escalation flags — knowing when to hand off to a human Storyteller

I want to design this system thoroughly before we begin writing Claude Code prompts. Key questions I want us to work through:

- What is the AI Storyteller's decision architecture? How does it decide what to do and when?
- What does it have access to? What game state does it read?
- How does it manage the patron contacts as persistent characters with memory and agenda?
- How does it handle The Correspondent specifically — the intentionally unreliable Malkavian patron?
- What are the hard limits of what it should handle vs. always escalate to humans?
- How does it generate atmospheric text that feels like a Storyteller and not a chatbot?
- How does it manage Wights — what triggers their appearance, what governs their behavior?
- How does the Vitae balance tracking connect to its narrative output?

Approach this the way we've designed everything else in the GDD — discuss first, make decisions, lock them in. Use widget questions where choices are bounded. Push back if something doesn't feel right.

### Context Notes

- One character per account. No permadeath — Torpor is the consequence floor.
- The players are accidentally maintaining the seal on the Methuselahs by rebuilding Kindred society. They don't know this yet.
- Politics is the game. Combat exists but is expensive. Domain control is the resource loop.
- The AI Storyteller must feel like a Storyteller, not a game engine. Atmospheric text matters enormously.
- The Harpy, Boon economy, and social ledger are fully designed — the AI Storyteller interfaces with these but does not control them directly.
- Vitae Sanctus is generated by healthy domain activity. Vitae Corrupta is generated by Kindred-on-Kindred violence including Torpor. Tracked per clan and per district. Imbalance triggers the Methuselah wake sequence.
- Chronicle cycles: the city name always stays Ephemera. History accumulates. Elder Fades become next cycle contacts.

### Design Principles We've Been Following

- Start with fiction, let mechanics emerge
- Every system should create emergent story, not just game outcomes
- Faithful to VtM source material, adapted where the medium demands
- No grinding — advancement through meaningful action
- The city should feel alive at 3am when no human Storyteller is watching
